Personal Independence Payment (PIP)

You have been invited to apply for Personal Independence Payment (PIP) using the new online service.

What to expect.

  1. You'll be asked some initial questions to make sure this service is right for you and so that we only ask you for information that's relevant to your situation.
  2. You'll be advised whether or not you may be eligilbe to apply. If you're not eligible we will tell you why and what else might help your situation.
  3. If you are eligible to apply you'll be asked to complete some further questions about your conditions and how they affect your daily life. You may also be asked to upload some supporting information such as a hospital report.
  4. DWP will look at your application to see if they can make a decision. You may be invited to a health assessment if there isn't enough information in your application.
  5. You will receive a decision in the post. If you disagree with the decision you can ask for it to be looked at again.

Before you start you will need.

  • your contact details, for exmaple telephone number
  • details of your health condition, for exmaple how long have you had it and how long do you expect it continue
  • your date of birth
  • your National Insurance number
  • your doctors or health workers name, address and contact number
  • dates of any time you have spent abroad
