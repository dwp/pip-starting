Difficulties moving around

For each difficulty tell us:

  • what the difficulty is
  • how often you have this difficulty – tell us about both good and bad days
  • why you have it, or how it relates to your condition
  • any aids, appliances or devices you use to get around including wheelchairs or mobility scooters
  • any help you get from another person
  • any help you feel you need but do not get

Examples

“I can only walk down one aisle of my local supermarket leaning on the trolley for support.”

“I am never able to walk more than 20 metres as quickly as most people do, because I can only walk very slowly without being at risk of falling. My carer physically supports me when we walk outside to make sure I do not fall.”
