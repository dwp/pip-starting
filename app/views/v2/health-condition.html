Is your ability to do everyday tasks or get around affected by any health conditions or disabilities you have?

By health conditions or disabilities, we mean physical, sight, hearing or speech difficulty, learning, cognitive, developmental or behavioural difficulties or mental health conditions.

What we mean by everyday tasks and getting around

Everyday tasks means:

  • preparing food
  • eating and drinking
  • managing your medicines or treatments
  • washing and bathing
  • using the toilet and managing incontinence
  • dressing and undressing
  • talking, listening and understanding
  • mixing with other people
  • managing money

Getting around means:

  • planning and following a journey
  • moving around

Select 'Yes, all the time or sometimes' if your ability to do any of these things is ever affected.
