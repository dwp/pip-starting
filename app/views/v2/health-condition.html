Do you have a health condition or disability that reduces your ability to do everyday tasks or get around?

A health condition or disability can be physical or mental.

What we mean by everyday tasks and getting around

Everyday tasks are:

  • preparing food
  • eating and drinking
  • managing treatments
  • washing and bathing
  • using the toilet and managing incontinence
  • dressing and undressing
  • talking, listening and understanding
  • mixing with other people
  • managing money

Getting around means:

  • planning and following a journey
  • moving around
