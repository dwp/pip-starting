Do you agree that your health and care professionals can share relevant information about your health conditions to support your claim?

The Department for Work and Pensions (DWP), or assessment providers approved by the Secretary of State, may need more information or evidence to process and make a decision on your claim.

My health and care professionals can give DWP relevant information to support my claim.
DWP will make a decision on my claim using the information they have already.

How DWP uses this information

If you agree, DWP will store your consent on your behalf. This means your health and care professionals will know they are legally allowed to share information about you.

DWP needs information about your health condition and how it affects you.

The law allows DWP to get, keep, and use this information for the purpose of processing and making a decision on:

  • your Personal Independence Payment claim
  • any other benefit youâ€™ve claimed
  • any other benefit you may claim or be awarded in the future
  • any mandatory reconsideration or appeal

If you change your mind

You can change your mind at any time. If you do, DWP will not ask your health and care professionals to share any more information.

If you want to give or withdraw your consent later, contact:

Phone: 0800 121 6279
Textphone: 0800 121 4493 if you have speech or hearing difficulties
