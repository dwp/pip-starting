Do you agree that your health professionals can share relevant information about your health conditions to support your application?

The Department for Work and Pensions (DWP), or assessment providers approved by the Secretary of State, may need more information or evidence to process and make a decision on your application.

My health professionals can give DWP relevant information to support my application.
DWP will make a decision on my application using the information they have already.

How DWP uses this information

If you agree, DWP will store your consent on your behalf. This means your health professionals will know they are legally allowed to share information about you.

DWP needs information about your health condition and how it affects you.

The law allows DWP to get, keep, and use this information for the purpose of processing and making a decision on your application and on any mandatory reconsideration or appeal that you've made.

If you change your mind

You can change your mind at any time. If you do, DWP will not ask your health professionals to share any more information.
